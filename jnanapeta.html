1.Kuvempu

Childhood:
1.Kuvempu was born on December 29, 1904, in Kuppalli, a village in the Shimoga district of Karnataka, India.
2.His father was a farmer and cattle herder, and Kuvempu grew up in a rural environment, which had a profound influence on his literary works.
3.He received his primary education in the Kannada language in the village school, and later went on to complete his higher education in Mysore.
Books written:
4. Kuvempu was a prolific writer who wrote primarily in the Kannada language.
He produced a large body of work that includes poetry, novels, plays, essays, and critiques.
Some of his most famous literary works include the poetry collections "Kavya Shree," "Kanakadasa Ramatirtha," and "Mangala," the novels "Malegalalli Madumagalu" and "Kanooru Heggadithi," and the play "Beralge Koral."
Awards:
7. Kuvempu was the first Kannada writer to be honored with the Jnanpith Award, India's highest literary honor, for his contributions to Kannada literature.
He was also a recipient of the Padma Vibhushan, India's second-highest civilian award, and several other national and state-level awards.
Philosophy and Social Reforms:
9. Kuvempu was a strong advocate for social and educational reforms, and his works often deal with themes of humanism, spirituality, and the relationship between humans and nature.
10.He was a prominent figure in the Kannada literary and cultural movements of the 20th century and played a key role in the establishment of the Kannada language as an official language of the Indian state of Karnataka.
